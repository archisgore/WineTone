# WineTone v2026.05.22 — The Camera, the Catalog, and a New Look

*Released May 22, 2026.*

If [v2026.05.21](v2026.05.21.md) was the day WineTone stopped feeling
like a research script and started feeling like a product,
v2026.05.22 is the day it started looking like one too. A complete
visual redesign, a phone-camera wine-label scanner, a browsable
catalog, an installable home-screen icon, a shareable palate identity
card, and the production-grade infrastructure underneath to make all
of it survive real traffic.

There is also a new branch in the deploy pipeline. If you're following
along with the source, code now goes to `stage` first
(staged at [staging.tone.wine](https://staging.tone.wine)), gets
clicked through, and then gets fast-forwarded to `main`. Both branches
remain Apache-2.0 and open.

---

## What's new for users

### A new visual design

Bold Inter typography, a single sticky one-line nav, white surfaces
with warm-gray cards, generous whitespace, and a refined burgundy-
plus-gold accent palette. The same content as before, presented like a
product rather than a developer's weekend project.

The "Pantone for wine" tagline now lives where it belongs — anchored
in the landing-page hero rather than repeated on every page.

### Snap a wine label with your phone

A new **📷 Scan** route at
[`/wines/scan`](https://tone.wine/wines/scan). Open it on your phone,
tap the dropzone, and your rear camera fires directly. A vision-
language model (Claude Haiku 4.5, ~2 seconds) extracts
`{producer, wine_name, vintage, variety, country, confidence}` as
JSON. We then fuzzy-match against the 164,069-wine corpus and either:

- show top catalog matches as clickable cards if the wine already
  exists in the corpus, or
- pre-fill the `/wines/new` form with the extracted fields so you
  can add it as a new wine with one tap.

Images are sent to the vision model in memory and never persisted.
The extracted JSON is the only thing that survives the request.
Rate-limited to 20 scans/hour per IP — costs ~$0.005 per scan,
which keeps the Anthropic budget bounded.

### Browse the whole catalog

[`/catalog`](https://tone.wine/catalog) is the new flat-browse view of
the 164K wines. Filter by country, variety, or by free-form text
search (Postgres full-text via the `tsv` column with `ts_rank`
relevance). Sort by most-labeled, most-recent, or A→Z. Cursor-
paginated 50 at a time so you can browse the whole corpus without
OFFSET-scanning the table on every click.

Click any card to land on the per-wine detail page at
`/wines/{wine_id}`. Shows the wine's facts, public-reviewer
aggregates (median points, median price, source-of-truth review
excerpt), and every WineTone user's label for that wine with
sentiment and author attribution.

### Install WineTone as an app

A `manifest.json`, three icon sizes, a service worker, and the
right `<link rel="manifest">` declarations turn the site into a
PWA. On iPhone Safari (Share → Add to Home Screen) or Android
Chrome (auto install prompt), the icon lands on your home screen
and opens full-bleed with no browser chrome — same look and feel as
a native app, no App Store gatekeeping. The service worker
cache-first's `/static/*` so repeat opens are instant.

### Shareable palate identity card

Every signed-in user now has a public, shareable palate report at
`/u/{user}/palate`. Five interpretable axes:

- **Savory ↔ Fruity** — umami / earth vs. ripe fruit
- **Structured ↔ Soft** — tannic grip vs. plush approachability
- **Old World ↔ New World** — terroir vs. fruit-forward extraction
- **Light ↔ Bold** — delicate refreshment vs. full-bodied intensity
- **Dry ↔ Off-dry** — austere lean vs. honeyed sweetness

Each axis is phrase-anchored: four hand-curated phrases per end
encoded once at startup, mean-pooled into centroids, then compared
against the user's wine-embedding centroid via cosine similarity.
A sigmoid maps the difference into a slider position. Confidence
rises with label count and saturates at five — the marker fades for
under-calibrated palates so the report doesn't overclaim.

Below the axes: a row of "distinctive vocabulary" pills (words the
user uses noticeably more than the global corpus, computed via
TF-relative-frequency) and their five most-recent labels.

A "Share" button at the bottom uses `navigator.share` on mobile
(opens the iOS / Android share sheet) and falls back to clipboard-
copy on desktop. The URL is fully public — anyone can visit, no
login required.

### Onboarding starter styles

New users used to land on a blank dashboard with "label five wines"
as the only instruction. Cold-start friction. The new
[`/onboarding`](https://tone.wine/onboarding) flow lets them pick one
of three archetypes — *Old-World Structurer*, *New-World Fruit-Lover*,
*Natural-Wine Adventurer* — and the dashboard then surfaces five
corpus wines that match the chosen style, ready to be labelled in
the user's own words. The starter section vanishes the moment the
user adds their first real label.

No synthetic labels, no projection seeding — the system narrows what
to label, the user still writes the description.

### Explain the recommendation

Every personalized recommendation now shows a one-sentence reason
underneath it: *"Recommended because you described 'savory funk' on
a natural Loire red, and this wine shares that profile."*

The math is straightforward — for each recommended wine, find the
user's positive label whose wine embedding is closest by cosine and
quote that label's description. The signal is already in the
projection; surfacing it builds trust and makes the calibration's
value visible.

### Inline label editing

The labels list on your dashboard now has an "edit" link next to
each row. Click it and the row swaps in place to a textarea +
sentiment radios + Save / Cancel. Saving HTMX-swaps the updated
list. The same idempotency that handles re-labelling a wine from
the search flow underpins this — the UPSERT path with the unique
`(user_id, wine_id)` constraint.

### Wines you've added to the catalog

Every user-profile page now lists the wines that user has
submitted via `/wines/new` or `/wines/scan`. Shown as a card grid
on your own dashboard and on visitors' views of your profile.
After you delete your account, the wines remain in the catalog as
attribution-less entries (your name is set to NULL via
`ON DELETE SET NULL` on the FK) — matching the privacy-policy
promise.

### Better dedup on wine submissions

Submitting the same wine twice now collapses cleanly even when
the inputs differ in case, whitespace, accents, or apostrophes.
*"Domaine Leroy 2015"*, *"DOMAINE LEROY 2015"*, *"Domaine Léroy
2015"*, and *"  domaine  leroy  2015  "* all map to a single row.
A new database-level `UNIQUE INDEX` on the canonicalized fields
backs up the application-side check, so even a hand-crafted bypass
INSERT gets rejected.

### One-time privacy disclaimer

The "Research prototype" banner that used to ride on every page is
gone. In its place: a one-time dismissible drawer for anonymous
visitors that explains the public-content posture, persists the
dismissal in `localStorage`, and never bothers them again. Signed-in
users don't see it at all — they've already accepted the posture
by signing in.

## Quality and accessibility

A full programmatic Lighthouse sweep — desktop and mobile — across
the seven anonymous pages.

| Category | Mobile | Desktop |
|---|---|---|
| **Accessibility** | **100** | 92-96 → **94-100** |
| Performance | 85-90 | 88-96 |
| Best Practices | 100 (prod) | 100 |
| SEO | 92 | 92 |

Specific fixes:

- **Color contrast.** The muted `--gray` used for secondary text was
  `#9c8d7e` against the cream background — a 2.99:1 contrast ratio,
  below WCAG AA's 4.5:1 minimum. Darkened to `#6b5e51` (5.7:1).
- **ARIA roles on HTMX swap targets.** Six dynamic-update divs had
  `aria-label` without a valid `role`, which axe flags as prohibited.
  Added `role="region"` (or `role="status"` for the fit-status
  indicator) so screen readers announce updates correctly.
- **`aria-live="polite"`** on every HTMX swap target — search
  results, label list, fit status, recommendations, /ask results,
  /vocab results.
- **Skip-to-main-content** link as the first body child, visually
  hidden until keyboard focus.
- **`lang="en"`** on `<html>`.
- **`Cache-Control: public, max-age=3600`** on `/static/*` — was
  unset, hurting repeat-visit performance and Lighthouse's cache
  audit.
- **Canonical URL** as an HTTP Link header (in addition to the
  in-HTML `<link rel="canonical">`) so HF Spaces' own canonical
  injection doesn't confuse Google.
- **Mobile-responsive layout** at ≤768px — header wraps, dashboard
  grids collapse to single column, tables become card-shaped, tap
  targets ≥ 44px per Apple HIG.

## What's new under the hood

### Production Clerk instance on `clerk.tone.wine`

The development Clerk instance (`united-stork-42.clerk.accounts.dev`)
is gone from production. Five DNS CNAMEs verified at Clerk
(`accounts`, `clerk`, two DKIM, mail), `pk_live_*` / `sk_live_*` /
`CLERK_WEBHOOK_SECRET` set as Space secrets, and `/healthz` confirms
`clerk_frontend: clerk.tone.wine`. Users sign in on our own subdomain
now — first-party cookies, no "Development mode" banner.

### Two-stage deploy pipeline

A new staging environment at `archisgore/winetone-staging` →
[`staging.tone.wine`](https://staging.tone.wine), pulling from a
`stage` git branch and pointed at a separate Neon database branch
plus the dev Clerk instance. Promotion is one command:
`git push origin stage:main` + factory-reboot the prod Space.

The full runbook is at
[`docs/runbooks/two-stage-deploy.md`](../runbooks/two-stage-deploy.md).
This was adopted after the v2026.05.21 → v2026.05.22 transition
caused real prod disruption (CSP-blocked CAPTCHA, user-creation 500)
that would have been caught on stage in five minutes of click-through.

### Full GDPR right-to-erasure with Clerk webhook

`/account/delete` now does a clean cascade: a Clerk Backend-API
delete first (so the user can re-sign-up cleanly), then `DELETE
FROM users` which fans out via `ON DELETE CASCADE` to labels,
projections, follow edges, label embeddings, and calibration
history.

A new `/webhooks/clerk` endpoint receives `user.deleted` events
fired when a user deletes themselves from the Clerk dashboard
directly, and does the same cascade. Webhook signature verification
via the `svix` library — any tampered or unsigned payload returns
400.

### Production-grade observability

- **Sentry** integration is wired up. `SENTRY_DSN` set as a Space
  secret. Abuse reports surface there too via
  `sentry_sdk.capture_message`.
- **Cloudflare Web Analytics** for cookieless, GDPR-friendly traffic
  counting. No banner needed.
- **UptimeRobot** monitor on `/healthz`. Pages on real outages —
  `/healthz` returns 503 when the DB is unreachable, not just on
  TCP failure.
- **Structured JSON logging** via `winetone/logging_config.py`. Each
  request gets a `request_id` threaded through a ContextVar and
  echoed in the `X-Request-Id` response header for cross-referencing.
- **`/healthz`** returns DB ping latency, Clerk JWKS reachability,
  and encoder load status — 503 on any failure.

### Security hardening

- **Rate limiting** via `slowapi` on every write path:
  60/min on follow/unfollow, 30/min on ask/query, 20/hour on
  calibrate-add and wine-submit, 10/hour on /report, 5/hour on
  account-delete. X-Forwarded-For-aware so the HF reverse proxy
  doesn't collapse all clients into one bucket.
- **HSTS, X-Frame, X-Content-Type-Options, Referrer-Policy,
  Permissions-Policy** middleware applied to every response.
- **Clerk-aware CSP** allowing the production Clerk frontend,
  Cloudflare Turnstile (Clerk's CAPTCHA), Google Fonts, and the
  unpkg HTMX bundle. No `unsafe-eval` would be cleaner but the
  HTMX bundle currently requires it.
- **Content moderation tripwire** (`winetone/moderation.py`) scans
  user-submitted text for URLs, casino/crypto spam, all-caps
  shouting, and PII patterns; surfaces to Sentry without blocking.
- **Admin abuse-report queue** at `/admin/reports`, gated by
  `ADMIN_CLERK_USER_ID` env var. Returns 404 (not 403) for
  unauthorized viewers so the route doesn't leak.

### Five new alembic migrations since the baseline

- `20260521_001_age_confirmation` — drinking-age self-attestation
- `20260522_002_user_labels_unique` — `UNIQUE(user_id, wine_id)`
  + dedupe historical duplicates
- `20260522_003_wines_submitted_by` — track who added each
  user-submitted wine (`ON DELETE SET NULL` so wines outlive
  account deletion)
- `20260522_004_canon_uniq` — DB-level `UNIQUE INDEX` on
  canonicalized fields, backstop against any future canonicalization
  drift
- `20260522_005_onboarding` — `users.onboarding_style` for the
  starter-style picker

### Operations runbooks

Ten new runbooks under [`docs/runbooks/`](../runbooks/) covering
Clerk production setup, encoder rotation, cost monitoring, DB
backup verification, email infrastructure, Cloudflare proxy
toggle, CDN static assets, and the new two-stage deploy pipeline.
Each is opinionated about whether the work is worth doing now or
deferring; together they cover the "what would a competent
operator need to know" surface.

### Encoder pre-warm

`@app.on_event("startup")` fires an asyncio task that loads the
sentence-transformer model and runs one inference at boot. The
first real user request after a Space restart no longer pays the
~17-second cold-load cost.

### Async-everything audit

The webhook handler's synchronous database write is now wrapped
in `run_in_threadpool`. Every other path that touches the DB lives
inside a sync `def` route handler, which FastAPI already runs in
a threadpool by default, so no further changes were needed.

## What's not in this release

For honesty about what we discussed and explicitly deferred:

- **A drinking journal** — letting users log "I drank this with
  whom on what date." Would convert WineTone from a "tool you visit
  when shopping" into a "thing you open after dinner." On the next
  product-work batch.
- **A wishlist / wines I want to try** — with notifications when
  someone you follow labels one. Deferred to pair with the journal.
- **A "Discover" mode** — surface wines your projection predicts
  you'd love but you haven't labelled. Distinct from `/ask` (no
  query needed). Deferred.
- **Native iOS / Android apps** — the PWA covers it. Native would
  be 4-6 weeks of work that doesn't validate the recommender thesis
  any further.
- **Retailer-pricing / inventory integration** — requires either
  scrapers (which we've ruled out as a class) or commercial
  partnerships. Defer until we have user volume to justify it.
- **Demographic analytics via GA4** — CF Analytics gives floor
  counts cookielessly. GA4's demographic inference doesn't have
  enough signal at our scale yet, and the consent-banner tax isn't
  worth paying until it does.

## How to try it

- Visit [https://tone.wine](https://tone.wine).
- Sign in with email / Google / GitHub. (Pick a pseudonymous
  username — everything you label is public.)
- Pick a starter style at `/onboarding` to get five suggested wines
  to label, or skip and label from a blank slate.
- Add labels in your own words. Five is enough to start; more
  sharpens the projection.
- Hit "Fit my taste profile."
- Open `/ask` or your dashboard's recommend box and type whatever
  you're in the mood for. Each personalized result now comes with
  a sentence explaining why.
- Check your palate identity card at `/u/{your-username}/palate`
  and share it if you like.
- On your phone, install WineTone to your home screen via
  Safari Share → Add to Home Screen (or Android Chrome's prompt).
  Tap the 📷 Scan icon next time you're standing in front of a
  confusing wall of bottles at the store.
